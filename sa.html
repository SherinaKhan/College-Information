SAT and ACT

SAT

The SAT is an standardized test used by most colleges and universities to make admissions decision. It assesses your abilities in three different sections: math, comprehensive reading, and writing. There is also an essay, but that section is optional. It is generally administed to students in the 11th grade. Most students tend to take the exam, both during the spring of their junior year or fall of their senior year. The purpose of the SAT is to measure your readiness for college, and provide colleges with one common data point that can be used to compare all applicants.

All JUNIORS must attend school to take the exam for FREE.

All SOPHOMORES must attend school to take the PSAT exam for FREE.

ALL 10th and 11th grade students are automatically registered by the DOE.

AFTER the March SAT, qualifying students may receive: 2 SAT fee waivers and 2 ACT fee waivers

Sign up to re-take another SAT after March if you want to get into a college with higher score.

ACT

The ACT is an entrance exam is also used by majority of colleges and universities to make admission decisions. It measures your capability in English, reading, math, science and writing. Majority of students take this exam during the spring of their junior year or fall of their senior year. This test is given for the same purpose as the SAT. If you register to take an ACT, you MUST register for the writing section. The writing section is REQUIRED for the ACT
